🔤 Typography
Primary Typeface — Headlines & Display
Neue Haas Grotesk or its free equivalent Inter (weight: 700–900)
→ Use for all headlines, hero text, nav. Tight letter-spacing (-0.03em). All caps for section labels.
Secondary Typeface — Body & UI
IBM Plex Mono
→ Used for stats, data, member handles, labels. The mono gives it a systems/terminal energy — reinforces the "execution machine" feel.
Accent Typeface — Subheads & Pull Quotes
Syne (weight: 700–800)
→ Used sparingly for pull quotes or section intros. Adds edge without being decorative.
Type Scale (desktop)

Hero: 72–96px / weight 900 / tracking -0.04em
H1: 48px / weight 800
H2: 32px / weight 700
Body: 16px / weight 400 / line-height 1.6
Label/Tag: 11px / ALL CAPS / weight 600 / tracking 0.12em / mono


🔷 Logo Concept
Primary Mark — Wordmark
HUSTLE HOUSE in Neue Haas Grotesk Extra Bold, full caps, tight tracking. Clean. Undecorated. The name is strong enough — no icon needed at this stage.
Secondary Mark — Monogram
HH — two blocky H's where the inner verticals merge into a single shared stroke. Feels structural, like a building or a circuit. Works as favicon, embossed stamp, badge.
Tertiary — Location Tag
A small label reads: KGP · EST. 2025 in IBM Plex Mono, 10px, ash grey — placed below the wordmark. Grounds it, adds authenticity.
Logo Don'ts

No drop shadows
No gradients on the mark
Never place on a white background (it kills the energy)
Never stretch, round, or add color fills


🧱 Design System Rules
Borders & Dividers
Thin 1px lines in #2A2A2A. Use them aggressively — they create that structured, technical grid feel.
Corners
Mostly sharp (0px radius). Very occasional 2–4px radius on small tags/badges only.
Spacing
Dense. This isn't a lifestyle brand with breathing room. Pack information with intention — like a dashboard, not a brochure.
Iconography
Line icons only. Stroke weight 1.5px. No filled icons. No emoji in UI.
Photography / Imagery
High contrast. Desaturated or monochrome. Candid over staged. Students working, screens, code, late nights — not handshakes and group photos. Amber color grade preferred.
Motion (if digital)
Fast cuts. No slow fades. Snap transitions. Stagger reveals on data/numbers.



SEO Things You Should Know Right Now
A few things specific to your setup:
Subdomains hurt SEO if misused. Google treats sayak.kgphustlehouse.com as a separate site. So all the authority you build on the main domain doesn't automatically flow to subdomains. Fix: add a canonical tag on every subdomain page pointing to kgphustlehouse.com/talent/sayak. That way Google consolidates authority on the main domain and the subdomain is just a UX/branding layer.
The talent directory page /talent is your most important SEO page after the homepage. It's the page that ranks for "hire IIT students" — make sure it has real copy, not just a filter + cards.
Each service page needs 600+ words of real content. Not filler. Actual explanation of what you do, how, for whom, what results look like. That's what ranks.
